Wheat paste installation

Wheat paste is a form of ephemeral art that allows us to do incredible things with three simple materials: paper, flour, and glue. Inspired by French wheat paste artist JR, we have taken Fortepan Iowa to the streets through wheat paste installations in cities and towns across Iowa. Fortepan Iowa's Director of Community Outreach, Isaac Campbell, has learned the art of wheat pasting by doing it--first on the streets of Budapest (using Fortepan photos), then as one of the many volunteers helping JR build The Secret of the Great Pyramid at the Louvre in 2019, and then all over Iowa. Having perfected his process, Isaac coordinated and installed every project you see below, and his work has received national and international attention. Look for more wheat paste installations throughout Iowa using Fortepan Iowa photos, and contact us to learn more about bringing wheat paste art to your community.
